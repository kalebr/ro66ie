## Great Games Come From Healthy, Well-Run Teams

With 30+ years of experience—spanning AAA blockbusters to lean independent studios—I've seen the same pattern repeat itself: talented people, capable of extraordinary work, held back by the structure around them. Production friction isn't usually a people problem. It's a process debt problem.

**Production Matters, LLC** exists to find it, name it, and help you fix it.

---

## The Achievement Formula

I believe team output is a solvable equation—but the solution is never "one size fits all." Every engagement starts with **The Achievement Formula**, a diagnostic lens for identifying exactly where your studio is leaking value:

$$P(\text{Achievement}) = \text{Skill} \times \text{Attitude} \times \text{Culture} \times \text{Tools} \times \text{Goal}$$

When a team isn't achieving what it should, one or more of these variables is broken or misaligned. The audit finds which ones, and why.

---

## What We Look For

A production audit covers the full range of problems that quietly kill momentum:

- **Scope creep and feature bloat** — work that expands without clear ownership or sign-off
- **Broken sprint and milestone planning** — schedules that don't reflect reality and teams that know it
- **Unclear ownership and accountability gaps** — decisions that fall through the cracks between disciplines
- **Poor communication across the team** — information that moves too slowly, ineffectively, or not at all
- **Crunch and unsustainable work practices** — short-term pressure compounding into long-term damage
- **Dysfunctional team culture** — the dynamics that no one wants to say out loud but everyone feels

If your team is smart and motivated but still struggling to ship—or shipping, but at a cost nobody wants to keep paying—these are the places to look.

## Methodology: Context Over Prescription

Many consultants arrive with a predetermined framework—Scrum, Kanban, SAFe, Waterfall—and try to fit your team into it. I do the opposite.

**The best process is the one that actually helps your team.** That means starting with the intentions behind your current mechanisms, understanding your unique team DNA, and shaping recommendations around how your studio actually works—not how a methodology says it should.

Three principles guide every engagement:

**Beyond Agile and Scrum.** Not every problem is solved by a standard framework. I draw from Lean (7 Wastes), SDT, custom workflows, and whatever specific levers will actually move the needle for your team.

**Information Velocity.** Production isn't just about managing tasks. It's about how information moves through an organization. Slow, unclear, or siloed information creates bad decisions at every level. I help you build systems that fix that.

**No Blame, Just Progress.** The goal of an audit is not to find someone to fault. It's to identify where value is being lost and provide a clear, actionable plan to recover it. We examine the cost and benefit of your processes, keep what's working, and reconsider what isn't.
